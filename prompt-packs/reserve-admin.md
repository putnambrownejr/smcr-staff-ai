## Admin Systems Quick Reference

### Drill Manager
- Captures IDT attendance → drives pay
- Errors in Drill Manager delay the entire pay cycle
- Unit admin must enter attendance accurately and on time
- Marines should verify their own entries when possible
- Common errors: wrong drill dates entered, Marine not on unit roster,
  duplicate entries, missing periods

### MROWS (Marine Reserve Order Writing System)
- Generates ADT/AT orders for Marine reservists
- Submit with enough lead time for approval chain
- **AT planning trigger: T-45 days** — MROWS submission deadline
- ADT requests need justification and funding source
- If orders aren't in MROWS, the Marine isn't getting paid for that duty
- Common errors: wrong duty type (IDT vs ADT vs AT), incorrect dates,
  missing funding citation, wrong unit identification code

### DTS (Defense Travel System)
- Travel authorizations (before travel) and vouchers (after travel)
- **T-30 days** — DTS authorization should be submitted
- **Post-drill voucher completion is the most common admin drop**
- Marines must have a DTS profile in the new unit's routing list
- Common errors: wrong accounting code, missing receipts over $75,
  unsigned vouchers, expired authorization

### MOL (Marine Online)
- Self-service portal for LES, OMPF, training records
- Marines should verify their information before each drill
- Check: rank, MOS, unit assignment, contact info, emergency data
- Access issues: usually a CAC/certificate problem (see General Marine pack)

### MCTFS / Unit Diary
- Authoritative system of record for all personnel status changes
- Unit Diary entries drive everything: pay, promotions, assignments
- If it's not in the Unit Diary, it didn't officially happen
- Common entries: gains/losses, promotions, duty status changes, awards
- Processing time: allow 3-5 business days for routine entries, longer
  for corrections

### MRRS / RHRP / PHA (Medical Readiness)
- Track dental class, PHA status, immunization readiness (IMR)
- All must be current — affects individual and unit readiness reporting
- **Dental Class 1 or 2** required (Class 3 = not ready)
- **PHA** — annual requirement, schedule early to avoid expiration gaps
- RHRP is the Reserve Health Readiness Program portal
- Medical readiness directly affects unit readiness percentages —
  one red Marine affects the whole unit's numbers

## Pay and Entitlements

### IDT Pay
- Driven by Drill Manager entries — no entry, no pay
- Pay grade + years of service determines rate
- 4 IDT periods per drill weekend (2 per day, typically)
- Check LES on MOL to verify correct payment
- If pay is wrong: first check Drill Manager entries, then contact S-1

### IDT Travel Reimbursement
- **MARADMIN 157/25: $750 cap per IDT travel reimbursement**
- Must have DTS authorization and voucher
- Receipts required for lodging, rental car, airfare (anything over $75)
- Mileage reimbursement for POV travel (check current GSA rate)
- File voucher promptly after each drill — delayed vouchers = delayed reimbursement

### AT/ADT Pay
- Paid via orders in MROWS
- Base pay + BAH + BAS for the duty period
- Verify orders are correct before executing (unit, dates, accounting code)
- If orders are wrong, fix them BEFORE the duty period — retroactive corrections
  are painful and can take months

### Pay Troubleshooting — Detailed Guide

| # | Problem | Likely Cause | Diagnostic Steps | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No pay after drill | Drill Manager entry missing | Check MOL LES for the pay period. Ask S-1 to verify Drill Manager entries. | S-1 enters correct attendance in Drill Manager, submits for processing. |
| 2 | Wrong pay amount | Incorrect pay grade or TIS in system | Compare MOL pay grade with actual rank. Check time-in-service calculation. | Submit UD correction for pay grade or TIS. May require supporting docs. |
| 3 | No travel reimbursement | DTS voucher not filed or stuck in routing | Log into DTS. Check voucher status: SUBMITTED, APPROVED, RETURNED? | If RETURNED: fix noted errors, resubmit. If not started: complete and submit. |

### DTS Step-by-Step — Voucher Completion

This is the most commonly dropped admin task. Here's the exact process:

**Before travel (Authorization):**
1. Log into DTS (defense.gov/dts)
2. Create a new authorization
3. Enter: travel dates, destination, per diem entitlement, transportation mode
4. Add estimated expenses: lodging, mileage, rental car, airfare
5. Select the correct accounting code (get this from your S-1 or admin chief)
6. Route for approval — must be APPROVED before you travel

**After travel (Voucher):**
1. Log into DTS
2. Open your existing authorization
3. Click "Create Voucher" (or "Amend" if adjusting dates)
4. Update actual dates if they changed
5. Enter actual expenses — must match receipts
6. Upload receipts for anything over $75 (lodging, airfare, rental car)
7. Verify per diem calculations (meals, incidentals)
8. Sign electronically
9. Route for approval

**Common DTS mistakes:**
- Wrong accounting code → voucher rejected, starts over
- Missing receipts → voucher returned, delayed reimbursement
- Not updating actual dates → calculation errors
- Forgetting to sign → voucher sits unprocessed
- Letting authorization expire before creating voucher → must recreate from scratch

**DTS tips:**
- Screenshot your approved authorization before traveling — if the system glitches,
  you have proof of approval
- Complete your voucher within 5 days of returning — this is when details are fresh
  and receipts haven't been lost
- If your voucher is returned, read the return notes carefully — fix exactly what
  was flagged and resubmit. Don't just resubmit unchanged.
- If you're stuck, ask your ODTA (Organizational Defense Travel Administrator) —
  every unit has one

## FitRep / Evaluation Cycle

### Officer FitReps — MCO 1610.7A

- **Reporting periods:** Occasion-driven (change of reporting senior, transfer, annual)
- **Annual FitRep:** Due within 30 days of the anniversary of the last report
- **Transfer report:** Due when either the Marine or the RS transfers
- The RS (Reporting Senior) writes; the MRO (Member Reported On) provides input
- **First counseling:** RS should counsel new MRO within 30 days of assuming duties
- Subsequent counseling at 6-month intervals (minimum)

### Enlisted Proficiency/Conduct Marks
- Semi-annual (June and December for SMCR)
- Score range: 0.0 to 5.0 in proficiency and conduct
- Marks below 4.0 in either category affect promotion eligibility
- Composite scores used for promotion boards

### FitRep Best Practices for the Reserve

- **For MROs (the person being evaluated):**
  - Provide your RS with a brag sheet before each counseling
  - Document accomplishments between drills — AT achievements, MarineNet courses,
    volunteer work, civilian skills that benefit the unit
  - Don't wait for AT to have the first counseling — do it at the first drill
  - If your RS hasn't counseled you within 30 days, request it

- **For Reporting Seniors:**
  - Counsel within 30 days of gaining a new MRO. Set expectations.
  - Counsel at least every 6 months. Keep notes from each counseling.
  - Reserve FitReps cover longer calendar periods with less observation time —
    be specific about what you observed during drill weekends and AT
  - Track your reporting senior profile — know how your marks compare to your
    historical average. Boards see relative marking, not absolute.
  - Don't write the FitRep from memory the week it's due. Keep a running log.

## Drill Prep Admin Calendar

### T-60 (Two Months Before Drill / AT)
- Navy attached personnel (corpsmen, chaplains): submit NROWS orders through NOSC
- Begin AT planning coordination with I&I
- Identify personnel requiring medical/dental appointments before drill
- Begin coordinating any ADT requirements

### T-45 (Six Weeks Out)
- MROWS orders submitted for any ADT/AT
- Verify all Marines' medical readiness (MRRS check)
- Begin DTS authorizations for Marines requiring travel
- Submit range requests and training area reservations (if not already done)

### T-30 (One Month Out)
- DTS authorizations should be submitted
- Drill notice distributed to all hands
- Confirm uniform of the day, training schedule, required gear
- Verify Drill Manager roster is current (gains, losses, status changes)
- Verify all FitRep counselings are current

### T-14 (Two Weeks Out)
- Final DTS authorizations submitted
- Verify MOL access and LES accuracy for all Marines
- Confirm drill site logistics: parking, billeting, chow
- Medical readiness red flags addressed
- Confirm Navy attached personnel have NROWS orders approved

### T-1 (Day Before Drill)
- Uniform, gear, documents ready
- Travel arranged, DTS authorization approved
- MarineNet annual training requirements checked
- Contact info for chain of command confirmed
- Review last drill's outstanding action items

### D-Day (Drill Weekend)
- Drill Manager attendance captured accurately — verify as Marines check in
- Admin corrections processed while Marines are present (this is your window)
- FitRep counseling for anyone due this period
- Awards routing, UD entries — do it now or wait 28 days
- Capture action items for post-drill follow-up with owners and deadlines

### D+5 (Post-Drill)
- DTS vouchers completed and submitted (chase these — it's the #1 drop)
- Drill Manager entries verified and locked
- Handoff notes updated for continuity
- Outstanding admin actions tracked with owners and deadlines
- Send a "post-drill admin" email with 3-5 must-do items and deadlines

## Awards

### Awards Processing — MCO 1650.19J

**Approval authorities:**
| Award | Approval Level |
|---|---|
| Certificate of Commendation | Battalion/Regimental CO |
| Letter of Appreciation | Any officer |
| NAM (Navy Achievement Medal) | O-6 (Colonel) |
| NavyCom (Navy Commendation Medal) | O-6 (Colonel) |
| MSM (Meritorious Service Medal) | General Officer |
| Legion of Merit | Secretary of the Navy |

### Citation Writing Guide

**Format:** Awards citations follow a specific structure. Each citation should:
1. Open with the period and the Marine's assignment
2. Describe 2-3 specific accomplishments with results
3. Close with impact on the unit/mission and reflect the Marine's character

#### Worked Example — NAM Citation

```
For meritorious service while serving as Company Gunnery Sergeant, Bravo Company,
2d Battalion, 25th Marines, 4th Marine Division, Marine Forces Reserve, from
June 2024 to June 2026. During this period, Staff Sergeant [NAME] distinguished
himself through exceptional performance of duty. He expertly managed the company's
training readiness, increasing T&R event completion from 62 percent to 91 percent
over two annual training cycles. He personally mentored 12 junior Marines in
preparation for promotion boards, resulting in 8 selections. His coordination of
the company's AT logistics plan at Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune reduced the
administrative reporting burden by 40 percent while improving fiscal accountability.
Staff Sergeant [NAME]'s outstanding initiative, professionalism, and unwavering
devotion to duty reflected great credit upon himself and were in keeping with the
highest traditions of the United States Marine Corps and the United States Naval
Service.
```

*Key rules:*
- Cite specific numbers and results — "increased from X to Y" is stronger than
  "significantly improved"
- The closing sentence is formulaic — use the exact wording above, adjusted for
  award level
- End-of-tour awards: submit 60-90 days before the Marine's transfer date
- Reserve awards often take longer due to the drill schedule — start early

### Common Awards Mistakes
- Submitting without endorsement letters from the chain of command
- Vague accomplishments ("performed duties in an exemplary manner" — what duties?)
- Missing the submission window for end-of-tour awards
- Not checking the current MARADMIN for any awards policy changes

## Navy Personnel Attached to Marine Units

Corpsmen, chaplains, and RPs assigned to Marine reserve units:

- Navy reservists' orders go through their **NOSC via NROWS**, NOT MROWS
- The Marine unit is the **gaining command** — you request, the NOSC executes
- **AT/ADT request flow:** Unit OpsO/S-1 writes request letter → NOSC submits
  in NROWS → CNRFC approves
- This chain is slower than MROWS. Start at **T-60 minimum** (vs T-45 for MROWS)
- Last-minute orders for Navy personnel are extremely difficult — the NOSC approval
  chain adds time the Marine system does not require
- Pay issues route through the **NOSC**, not the Marine S-1
- Maintain a separate tracker:

| Name | Rate | NOSC | NROWS Status | AT Dates | Order Req Date | Notes |

| HM3 Smith | HM | NOSC Chicago | Submitted | 15-28 JUL | 15 MAY | Follow up w/ NOSC 01 JUN |
