## Staff Product Templates

### 5-Paragraph Order (OPORD) — MCWP 5-10

1. **Situation** — enemy, friendly, attachments/detachments
2. **Mission** — who, what, when, where, why (5 W's) — ONE SENTENCE
3. **Execution** — commander's intent, concept of operations, tasks to subordinate
   units, coordinating instructions
4. **Admin & Logistics** — supply, transportation, services, personnel, CASEVAC
5. **Command & Signal** — command relationships, signal plan

**WARNORD** uses abbreviated structure — alert the unit, don't write the full order yet.

**FRAGORD** contains changes to existing order ONLY — do not restate unchanged elements.

#### Worked Example — OPORD Paragraph 3 (Execution)

```
3. EXECUTION

   a. Commander's Intent. 2d Platoon seizes OBJ HAWK to deny enemy observation
      of MSR TAMPA. End state: OBJ HAWK secured, enemy OP destroyed or displaced,
      MSR TAMPA open to logistics traffic.

   b. Concept of Operations. Two phases.
      Phase 1 (FIX): 1st Squad establishes a support-by-fire position on Hill 42
      to suppress the enemy OP. 2d Squad moves to assault position via covered
      route along Draw ALPHA.
      Phase 2 (ASSAULT): On signal from the platoon commander, 2d Squad assaults
      OBJ HAWK from the south while 1st Squad shifts fires. 3d Squad is reserve,
      prepared to reinforce either squad or exploit success to the north.

   c. Tasks to Subordinate Units.
      (1) 1st Squad: Establish SBF position on Hill 42 NLT 261345ZJUN26. Suppress
          enemy OP on OBJ HAWK. Shift fires on signal.
      (2) 2d Squad: Move to assault position via Draw ALPHA. Assault OBJ HAWK on
          signal. Clear from south to north.
      (3) 3d Squad: Reserve. Priority: reinforce 2d Squad.

   d. Coordinating Instructions.
      (1) Phase Line RED: Hill 42. Phase Line BLUE: 300m south of OBJ HAWK.
      (2) H-Hour: 261400ZJUN26.
      (3) Assault signal: Green star cluster.
      (4) Shift fire signal: Red star cluster.
      (5) PIR: Enemy reinforcement from the east.
```

*Notice:* The concept paints a picture ("fix and assault"), tasks are specific with
times and locations, and coordinating instructions prevent fratricide.

### Decision Brief

- Problem statement — what decision is needed and why now
- Options — 2-3 real alternatives with pros/cons for each
- Recommendation — your pick and why
- Decision requested — what exactly you need the commander to decide

### SITREP

- DTG and reporting unit
- Significant activity since last report
- Personnel status (strength, casualties, replacements)
- Logistics status (supply levels, equipment readiness)
- Commander's assessment
- Requests/recommendations

### After Action Report (AAR)

- Event/exercise name, dates, participants
- Training objectives and whether each was met
- **Sustains** — what worked and should be repeated
- **Improves** — what didn't work and specific corrective actions
- Corrective actions tied to training objectives (who, what, when)

### Synchronization Matrix

Time-phased grid showing tasks by element:

| Time/Phase | Maneuver | Fires | Intel | Logistics | Comms |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 (0600-0800) | 1st Plt moves to LD | Prep fires on OBJ | Confirm enemy pos | Ammo pre-staged at RP1 | Nets up NLT 0545 |
| Phase 2 (0800-1000) | Assault OBJ HAWK | Shift fires south | Report BDA | CASEVAC staged | Switch to alt freq on order |
| DP 1 (on order) | Commit reserve? | CAS on station? | Enemy reinforce? | Resupply priority? | Report to higher |

*Notice:* Decision points have questions, not answers — they mark where the
commander must decide, not where the staff already decided for them.

### CCIR (Commander's Critical Information Requirements)

- **PIR** — Priority Intelligence Requirements (enemy-focused)
- **FFIR** — Friendly Force Information Requirements (own-force status)
- **EEFI** — Essential Elements of Friendly Information (what to protect)

Must be commander-approved, not staff-generated without endorsement.

### Naval Letter — MCO 5216.20B, SECNAV M-5216.5

#### Worked Example

```
                                                        1000
                                                        Ser ####
                                                        DD Mon YYYY

From:  Commanding Officer, Bravo Company, 2d Battalion, 25th Marines
To:    Commanding Officer, 2d Battalion, 25th Marines

Subj:  REQUEST FOR ADDITIONAL ANNUAL TRAINING DAYS

Ref:   (a) MCO 1001R.1L w/CH-2
       (b) FY26 AT Execution Order (BN OPORD 01-26)

Encl:  (1) Bravo Company AT Training Plan
       (2) Cost Estimate

1.  Per reference (a), Bravo Company requests authorization for two (2)
additional AT days (16 total) to support live-fire training requirements
outlined in enclosure (1).

2.  Background.  The current 14-day AT allocation provides insufficient
range time to complete T&R event requirements for all three rifle
platoons. Reference (b) prioritizes combined arms integration, which
requires dedicated range days beyond current allocation.

3.  Cost estimate at enclosure (2) reflects per diem and ammunition
costs only. No additional transportation required.

4.  Point of contact is the undersigned at (555) 555-1234 or
j.smith@usmc.mil.


                                    J. M. SMITH
                                    Captain, USMCR
```

*Key format rules:*
- From/To use full unit designation
- References are listed in order of first citation
- Paragraphs are numbered
- Purpose statement goes in paragraph 1
- Signature block: name in caps, rank, service

### Point Paper

One-page single-issue summary:

- **Discussion** — key facts, considerations, options

Internal working document — same structure as a naval letter but less formal.

## ORM / Risk Management Products

### ORM Worksheet — Worked Example

| # | Hazard | Severity | Probability | Risk (Raw) | Controls | Residual Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Heat injury during summer FEX | Critical | Likely | HIGH | Enforce hydration schedule, WBGT monitoring hourly, shade rest plan, corpsman with IV capability | MEDIUM |
| 2 | Live-fire range accident | Catastrophic | Seldom | HIGH | RSO present, PMI/coaches 1:4, brass checks, no-go criteria enforced | MEDIUM |
| 3 | Vehicle accident during convoy | Critical | Occasional | HIGH | TC/driver qual verified, 25mph speed limit in training area, vehicle spacing 100m, ground guide at obstacles | MEDIUM |
| 4 | Slip/fall on unimproved terrain | Marginal | Likely | MEDIUM | Buddy teams, terrain recon, first-aid kit per squad | LOW |

**Severity scale:**
- **Catastrophic:** Death, permanent disability, loss of major equipment
- **Critical:** Permanent partial disability, major equipment damage, significant mission degradation
- **Marginal:** Minor injury, minor equipment damage, some mission degradation
- **Negligible:** First-aid level injury, minimal impact

**Probability scale:**
- **Frequent:** Continuously experienced
- **Likely:** Will occur several times
- **Occasional:** Will occur sometime
- **Seldom:** Unlikely but possible
- **Unlikely:** Can assume will not occur

### No-Go Criteria

Conditions that **stop the event** before or during execution. These are non-negotiable
— if any condition is met, the event halts until corrected.

**Example no-go criteria for a company FEX:**
- Lightning within 5 NM of training area
- Heat category V (WBGT > 90°F) without approved mitigation
- RSO/safety officer not present at live-fire events
- Corpsman not present (or CASEVAC not confirmed for remote training areas)
- Primary communications with higher HQ inoperative
- Any vehicle not PMCSed prior to movement
- Any Marine on the firing line without PMI qualification verified

### Residual-Risk Decision Note

After controls are applied, what risk remains? Who accepts it?

| Risk Level | Risk Acceptance Authority |
|---|---|
| Low | Company Commander or event OIC |
| Medium | Battalion Commander |
| High | Regimental or Division Commander |
| Extremely High | First General Officer in the chain |

Safety-specific items for inclusion in rehearsals and pre-execution briefs:

## Writing Coach Rules

**Common staff writing failures to catch:**
- Burying the recommendation below 3 paragraphs of background
- Missing the audience — writing for peers when the reader is the commander
- Assumptions hiding in the prose instead of being stated explicitly
- Products that describe the process instead of answering the question
- Using passive voice to avoid naming who is responsible
  ("It was determined" → "The S-3 determined")
- Presenting one option disguised as three (COAs that differ only in timing
  are one COA with variants, not three COAs)

**Good staff-product habits:**
- Lead with the problem, decision, or recommendation
- Use headings that reflect thought, not paperwork
- Keep each paragraph doing one job
- Make assumptions and caveats explicit
- Build slides and briefs around what the commander must grasp quickly
- If the product is clean but the logic is weak, fix the logic first
- The appendix exists — use it. Put supporting data in the back, not the body.

**Pressure these questions on every product:**
1. Who is the audience?
2. What decision or understanding is required?
3. What belongs in the main body, and what belongs in backup?
4. What claim needs evidence or a source note?
5. What can be cut without losing the point?

**Briefing-specific coaching:**
- The commander has 3 minutes of attention for your topic. What do they need
  to grasp in that window?
- Slides are not documents. If your slide has more than 15 words, it's a
  document pretending to be a slide.
- Brief the decision, not the journey. Nobody needs to hear about your
  planning process — they need to hear your recommendation and why.
- Anticipate the 3 questions the commander will ask. Have the answers ready
  on backup slides, not in your head.

## Interagency Knowledge (current as of 2026)

For FHADR, humanitarian, and interagency products:

- **USAID no longer exists** (dissolved 2025). Use **DoS/BHR** (Department of State,
  Bureau of Humanitarian Assistance) as the lead humanitarian agency.
- **JP 3-29** (Foreign Humanitarian Assistance): substitute DoS/BHR wherever
  USAID/BHA appears.
- The **DART** concept may continue under DoS/BHR as a "forward coordination team" —
  confirm current terminology before using in products.
- Do not reference USAID as an active organization. Historical references are fine
  for context (e.g., "formerly USAID/BHA, now DoS/BHR").
