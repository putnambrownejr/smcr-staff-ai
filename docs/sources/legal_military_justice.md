# Military Justice And SJA Grounding

This note captures the public-source spine the legal lane should use for advisory military-justice issue-spotting.
The target is not a lawyer impersonation. The target is a command-support assistant that knows when to ask the
right question, when to shut up, and when to route a matter into the real legal channel.

## What To Ingest

- The command-facing term should usually be `SJA`, not generic `JAG`, when the user is asking for Marine command
  support.
- The legal lane should be strongest at:
  - NJP / Article 15 issue-spotting
  - Reserve-component and jurisdiction questions
  - courts-martial process awareness
  - routing to the right legal office
  - protecting records, rights, and timing discipline
- The legal lane should never pretend to:
  - give case-specific legal advice
  - predict case outcomes
  - replace SJA, defense, VLC, or trial counsel

## Staff-Construct Implications

- The staff should learn to produce clean legal handoff packages instead of sprawling narrative dumps.
- The XO and S-3 lanes should understand when the staff is entering real legal terrain and stop freelancing.
- Reserve status, RECC/EAS timing, drill status, and jurisdiction questions should trigger extra caution automatically.
