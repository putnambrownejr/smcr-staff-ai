# TBS And SOI Training Grounding

This note captures public-source material from Training Command, The Basic School, and School of Infantry that is
worth keeping as staff and training grounding. The goal is not to clone schoolhouse POIs. The goal is to absorb the
parts that should shape how the repo thinks about basic warfighting development, infantry familiarization, and how
training is scoped for Marines who are not full-time infantry professionals.

## What These Add

- TBS matters because it tells us what every Marine officer is supposed to understand at baseline:
  - professional knowledge
  - leadership
  - rifle-platoon warfighting mindset
  - common tactical vocabulary
- SOI matters because it reminds us what the entry and advanced infantry pipeline is actually trying to produce:
  - Marines trained and evaluated in field craft and MOS tasks
  - Combat Instructors who teach, mentor, and lead
  - basic qualification before fleet refinement, not instant mastery
- Training Command matters because it reinforces the standard we want throughout the repo:
  - standards-based training
  - evaluation
  - skills that are delivered to the operating forces
  - training designed and assessed on purpose

## Staff-Construct Implications

- The S-3 and training lanes should distinguish clearly between:
  - familiarization
  - qualification
  - advanced leader development
- For non-03 Marines doing infantry-flavored training, the repo should prefer:
  - basic tactical vocabulary
  - leader control
  - safety
  - repetition
  - AAR discipline
  - honest scope reduction
  over cinematic complexity
- The repo should continue to treat TBS/SOI-derived material as:
  - development and framing input
  - not authoritative local SOP
  - not a substitute for qualified 03xx leaders or formal schoolhouse instruction
