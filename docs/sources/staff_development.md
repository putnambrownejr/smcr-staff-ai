## Notes

Use this source set to tune:

- decisiveness
- commander decision framing
- cross-staff synchronization
- operational-planning-team discipline
- assumption logs and decision logs
- writing and briefing discipline
- critical reading and critical thinking under uncertainty
- practicality under time pressure
- willingness to cut weak ideas

## EWS / CSC Takeaways Worth Ingesting

- EWS is still the best public reference for the company-grade baseline we want:
  - lead with Marine Corps values
  - fight with a maneuver-warfare mindset
  - employ a MAGTF in a naval expeditionary environment
  - demonstrate MOS credibility
- EWS also makes the practical application bias explicit: doctrinal foundation, exchange of practical experience,
  and numerous planning exercises.
- CSC pushes the staff farther up the ladder:
  - critical thinkers
  - innovative problem solvers
  - ethical leaders
  - commanders and staff officers able to work in Marine, joint, interagency, intergovernmental, and multinational
    contexts
- CSC curriculum is especially useful for shaping how the staff should think:
  - leadership in the profession of arms
  - joint and Marine Corps operations
  - Marine Corps Planning Process
  - complex operational problem solving and design
  - capstone planning exercise against a thinking enemy in an ambiguous environment
- CSC's Preparatory Knowledge Program is a useful warning against shallow staff work:
  - the school expects critical thinking, critical reading, writing, research, and MCPP refresh before arrival
  - that means our staff construct should not just produce products; it should force reasoning, reading, framing,
    and explanation

## Staff-Construct Implications

The staff should increasingly behave like:

- an operational planning team that keeps visible assumption, decision, and risk logs
- a field-grade staff that can connect Marine planning to joint and external stakeholders
- a team that can write, brief, defend, and refine products instead of only generating them
- a command-post rhythm that uses running estimates, CUBs, CPBs, TDGs, and AARs as one learning loop

Do not use these sources to imitate biography, private speech, or invented personal anecdotes. The aim is
professional judgment and staff maturity, not cosplay.
