# EWS And Command-And-Staff PME Notes

This note captures the public PME material most worth turning into agent behavior and staff-construct
improvements. The point is not to imitate a schoolhouse. The point is to use official curriculum signals to make the
staff bench think more like educated company-grade and field-grade Marines.

## Official Sources

- Expeditionary Warfare School Resident Curriculum
  - Official URL: https://www.usmcu.edu/Colleges-and-Schools/Expeditionary-Warfare-School/Resident/Curriculum/
  - Why it matters: public statement of the company-grade baseline for leadership, warfighting mindset, MAGTF
    employment, and MOS credibility.

- Command and Staff College
  - Official URL: https://www.usmcu.edu/Colleges-and-Schools/Command-and-Staff-College/
  - Why it matters: public statement of intermediate-level PME outcomes, especially critical thinking, ethical
    leadership, command-and-staff competence, and work in joint or external environments.

- Command and Staff College Preparatory Knowledge Program
  - Official URL: https://www.usmcu.edu/Colleges-and-Schools/Command-and-Staff-College/Preparatory-Knowledge-Program/
  - Why it matters: shows what CSC expects students to refresh before arrival: planning, critical thinking, critical
    reading, writing, research, and joint/Marine Corps operations.

- MCWP 5-10 Marine Corps Planning Process
  - Official URL: https://www.marines.mil/News/Publications/MCPEL/Electronic-Library-Display/Article/900553/mcwp-5-10/
  - Why it matters: doctrinal backbone for MCPP and honest compressed planning.

- MCDP 5 Planning
  - Official URL: https://www.marines.mil/News/Publications/MCPEL/Electronic-Library-Display/Article/899841/mcdp-5/
  - Why it matters: planning as learning, decision support, and adaptation under uncertainty.

- MCTP 3-30A Command and Staff Actions
  - Official URL: https://www.marines.mil/News/Publications/MCPEL/Electronic-Library-Display/Article/899747/mctp-3-30a/
  - Why it matters: staff-action rhythm, command-post discipline, and cross-staff synchronization.

## What EWS Should Do To The Staff

- Preserve a company-grade bias for:
  - clarity
  - initiative
  - MAGTF literacy
  - MOS credibility
  - practical application
- Keep training and planning advice grounded in:
  - doctrinal fundamentals
  - numerous practical exercises
  - conference-group style feedback
  - realistic reserve constraints

## What CSC Should Do To The Staff

- Add field-grade habits:
  - critical reading before product generation
  - visible assumption, decision, and risk logs
  - better writing and briefing discipline
  - cross-functional staff synchronization
  - joint and external-context awareness
  - leadership of an operational planning team, not just authorship of slides
- Force staff products to answer:
  - what problem is actually being solved
  - what decision is required
  - what assumptions still carry the plan
  - what adjacent section support is required
  - what branch or sequel should already be anticipated

## Additions Worth Making To The Staff Construct

- OPT facilitator / mission-analysis controller
  - Keeps the staff in the right MCPP step
  - Maintains assumption, decision, and question logs
  - Prevents premature order-writing

- Red-team / assumptions challenge role
  - Tests weak logic, unexamined assumptions, and false COA differences
  - Useful during mission analysis, wargaming, and CPB prep

- Assessment and learning role
  - Connects MET/METL, measures, AARs, and next-drill follow-through
  - Prevents AARs from becoming unowned observations

- Writing and briefing coach
  - Helps the staff produce cleaner commander products
  - Reinforces audience, structure, evidence, and brevity

- Joint / interagency frame advisor
  - Especially useful above company level
  - Keeps CSC-style external context from being ignored or hand-waved

## Guidance For Agents

These PME sources should make the staff:

- reason better before drafting
- brief more clearly
- challenge assumptions earlier
- coordinate across sections more deliberately
- treat planning as a command-learning activity, not a template-filling drill
