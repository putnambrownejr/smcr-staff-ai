# Echelon and MAGTF Organization Guide

Marine Corps organization uses two overlapping logics: relatively stable **service
echelons** (squad through division) and **task-organized MAGTFs** (MEU, MEB, MEF,
SPMAGTF) that combine command, ground, aviation, and logistics elements under one
commander.

This guide explains both and how echelon context changes staff behavior, products,
and coordination requirements across the application's agents.

## Echelon Levels

### Platoon

**Typical structure:** 3 rifle squads + platoon HQ (~40 Marines). Squad = 3 fire
teams of 4 Marines (13 per squad under Force Design).

**Staff implications:** Minimal staff structure. Platoon commander and platoon
sergeant handle nearly all functions. No formal staff products. Focus on immediate
tactical concerns.

### Company / Battery / Troop

**Typical structure:** 3 rifle platoons + weapons platoon + HQ platoon (~180-200
Marines for a rifle company).

**Staff implications:** Limited organic staff. XO doubles as primary staff officer.
Focus shifts to company-level SOPs, readiness reporting up to battalion, and
maintenance/supply at the lowest formal level. The company is the basic maneuver
element and can operate semi-independently when reinforced.

### Battalion / Squadron (default)

**Typical structure:** 3 rifle companies + 1 weapons company + 1 H&S company
(~900-1,200 Marines). Weapons company adds medium mortars, antiarmor, heavy machine
guns. Full S-staff.

**Staff prefix:** S- (S-1 through S-6)

This is the first echelon with a complete staff structure:
- **S-1:** DTS, awards, FitReps, unit diary, personnel accounting
- **S-2:** IPB at battalion level, threat assessment for training areas
- **S-3/OpsO:** Training plans, range scheduling, drill coordination, synchronization
- **S-4:** Tactical logistics — maintenance readiness, supply requests, class I-IX
- **S-6:** Tactical comms, PACE plans, battalion network
- **SJA:** Issue-spotting, military justice (predominant in garrison)
- **Surgeon:** CASEVAC planning, training-safe medical support

**Products:** Training plans, LOGSTATs, PERSTATs, FRAGORDs.

**Force Design note:** Modernized battalions receive loitering munitions, enhanced
small UAS, signature-management tools, and EW/SIGINT capability. Units are expected
to operate more autonomously with degraded communications.

### Regiment / MEU / Wing / MLR

**Typical structure:**
- *Regiment:* 3-4 infantry battalions + HQ (~2,500-3,500 Marines)
- *MEU:* Reinforced infantry battalion (BLT) + composite squadron + CLB + CE (~2,200
  Marines). Certified for ~15 days self-sustainment.
- *MLR:* Littoral combat team + littoral anti-air battalion + CLB (~1,800-2,000
  Marines). Optimized for EABO, maritime sensing, anti-ship fires.
- *SPMAGTF:* Tailored CE/GCE/ACE/LCE for a specific mission. Size varies.

**Staff prefix:** S- (but scope expands significantly)

Staff sections gain more depth (assistant S-3, future ops cell, dedicated collection
management). Products include regimental-level estimates and MEU-specific planning
(ARG/MEU integration).

**MEU context:** The MEU is a combined-arms MAGTF with organic ACE (MV-22B, CH-53,
AH-1Z, UH-1Y, F-35B), GCE (BLT), LCE (CLB), and CE. Employment is sea-based,
crisis-response oriented. Command relationship involves the ARG/MEU relationship
with the Navy (CATF/CLF).

**MLR context:** The Marine Littoral Regiment is not a traditional infantry regiment.
It is a stand-in naval formation optimized for expeditionary advanced base operations,
maritime sensing, and anti-ship fires (NMESIS).

### Division / Group / MEB / MEF

**Typical structure:**
- *Division:* 3 infantry regiments + artillery regiment + combat support battalions
  (~20,000+ Marines)
- *MEB:* Reinforced infantry regiment + aircraft group(s) + MLG components + CE
  (~3,000-20,000 Marines). ~30-day sustainment.
- *MEF:* 1+ divisions + aircraft wings + logistics groups + permanent CE
  (~45,000-47,000 for I and II MEF). Principal warfighting organization.

**Staff prefix:** G- (G-1 through G-9)

Scope shifts from tactical to operational. Products are theater-level: OPLANs, force
deployment plans, strategic comms. Staff sections are large with multiple cells
(current ops, future ops, plans). Command relationships involve joint constructs
(COCOM/OPCON/TACON/ADCON).

Unique staff sections at this echelon:
- **G-8:** Programming and budgeting (not present at battalion)
- **G-9:** Civil-military operations at scale

## How Echelon Changes Staff Behavior

| Functional area | Battalion (S-) | Regiment/MEU | Division/MEF (G-) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operations | Company-level training, range scheduling | Battalion synchronization, future ops | Operational planning, joint coordination |
| Fires | Mortars, call for fire | FSCC — fire support coordination | FECC — MAGTF-level fires, targeting |
| Intelligence | IPB, threat assessment | Collection management, all-source | National-level coordination, CI/HUMINT at scale |
| Logistics | Maintenance, supply, class I-IX | Distribution, MSR/ASR planning | Theater logistics, strategic lift |
| Communications | Battalion network, PACE | Regimental/MEU networks | Enterprise networks, cyber defense |
| Legal | Military justice (garrison priority) | Operational law surges | International law, authorities, targeting review |
| Medical | CASEVAC, training safety | Health service support | Medical battalions, theater HSS |
| Civil affairs | Minimal | Civil reconnaissance, partner engagement | Theater civil-military operations |

## Command Relationships

| Context | Vocabulary | When it applies |
|---|---|---|
| Within a MAGTF | **Command** and **support** | Internal MAGTF coordination |
| Joint construct | **COCOM / OPCON / TACON / ADCON** | Joint operations, combatant commands |
| Amphibious ops | **CATF / CLF** relationship | ARG/MEU ops, ship-to-shore movement |

At battalion and below, command relationships are usually straightforward (organic
command). At regiment and above, especially MEU/MEB/MEF, the command relationship
architecture must be deliberately designed and specified in the initiating directive.

## Force Design 2030 by Echelon

- **Battalion and below:** Smaller signatures, more organic sensors (sUAS, EW/SIGINT),
  loitering munitions, counter-sUAS. Units operate more autonomously.
- **Regiment/MLR:** The MLR is the signature Force Design formation — optimized for
  EABO, anti-ship fires (NMESIS), maritime sensing.
- **MEF/Division:** Emphasis on distributed maritime operations, naval integration,
  contested logistics, joint kill webs. MEFs deploy by echelon rather than monolithic.

## Notes

- **Default to battalion** when echelon is unspecified — it's the most common SMCR context.
- **Personnel numbers are typical ranges**, not exact T/O&E. MCRP 1-10.1 no longer
  contains specific structure information.
- **UNCLASSIFIED only.** Echelon context is organizational structure — no unit-specific
  details, real task org, deployment information, or classified force structure data.
