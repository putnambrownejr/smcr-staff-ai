# Reserve Officer PME Planning Guide (UNCLASSIFIED)

## Why PME Matters in the Reserves

Professional Military Education (PME) completion is a discriminator for promotion boards. Unlike active duty, reserve officers must actively seek out and schedule PME around civilian careers — nobody will hand you a school seat.

## PME by Grade

### Second Lieutenant (O-1) → First Lieutenant (O-2)
- **Required**: The Basic School (TBS) — completed before or shortly after commissioning
- **MOS school**: Completed after TBS
- **Timeline**: Promotion to 1stLt is largely time-based (2 years TIG)
- **Action**: If you haven't completed TBS/MOS school, this is priority one

### First Lieutenant (O-2) → Captain (O-3)
- **Required**: Expeditionary Warfare School (EWS)
- **Options**: Resident (5 months at Quantico), seminar (weekend/evening program), or distance education
- **Reserve reality**: Distance or seminar is most common; resident is career-enhancing but requires long orders
- **Timeline**: Complete before captain promotion board looks at your record
- **Action**: Enroll early — distance programs have wait lists. Check MARADMIN for annual enrollment windows

### Captain (O-3) → Major (O-4)
- **Required**: Command and Staff College (CSC) or equivalent Joint PME Phase I (JPME-I)
- **Options**: Resident (1 year at Quantico), seminar, distance, or sister-service equivalent (Army CGSC, etc.)
- **Reserve reality**: Seminar programs run at various locations; some are near reserve centers. Distance is an option but resident is a strong discriminator.
- **Timeline**: Should be complete or in progress when the major promotion board convenes
- **Action**: Apply for seminar seats through your career planner; check MARADMIN for application windows
- **JPME note**: CSC satisfies JPME-I. If you attend a joint school, verify it's on the OPMEP-approved list.

### Major (O-4) → Lieutenant Colonel (O-5)
- **Required**: Top Level School (TLS) / JPME-II or equivalent
- **Options**: Various war college programs (resident, seminar, distance, fellowship)
- **Reserve reality**: War college seminar programs exist for reservists; some are hybrid (one week resident + distance)
- **Action**: Competitive selection — apply when eligible and build a strong record leading up to it

## How to Find and Apply for PME

1. **Check current MARADMINs** — search for "PME" and "professional military education" on the MARADMIN site
2. **Talk to your career planner** — they track quotas and application deadlines
3. **MarineNet** — distance education programs are administered here
4. **Unit S-3 / Training Officer** — they should know about upcoming seminar seats
5. **MARFORRES G-3 Training** — controls many reserve PME allocations

## PME Scheduling Tips for Reservists

- **Start early.** If you wait until you're "in zone" for promotion, you're too late for many programs.
- **Use AT/ADT days strategically.** Some PME courses can be covered by ADT orders.
- **Employer communication.** USERRA protects you, but early notice to your employer reduces friction.
- **Sister-service opportunities.** Army, Navy, and Air Force PME programs can satisfy requirements — verify equivalency with your career planner first.
- **Document everything.** Ensure PME completion is reflected in your OMPF and MOL training record. If it's not in the system, the board won't see it.

## Non-Required but Career-Enhancing Education

| Program | Value | Access |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Martial Arts Instructor (MAI/MAIT) | Shows investment in Marines; useful at company level | Courses run at major installations |
| Mountain Warfare / Jungle Warfare | Specialized skill; great for operational credibility | Schools at MWTC Bridgeport / JWTC Okinawa |
| Airborne / Air Assault | Joint qualification; shows initiative | Army schools, apply through unit |
| MOS-specific advanced courses | Deepens technical expertise in your field | Check MOS roadmap with career planner |
| Joint exercises | Broadens perspective; good for JPME context | Volunteer through S-3 |
