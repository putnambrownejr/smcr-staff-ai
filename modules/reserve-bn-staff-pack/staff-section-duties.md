# Battalion Staff Section Duties — Reserve Context (UNCLASSIFIED)

## What's Different in the Reserves

Reserve battalion staffs operate under constraints that active duty staffs don't face:

- **28-day gaps** between drills break continuity — handoff notes are the only bridge
- **Skeleton I&I** — 5-7 active duty Marines run the shop between drills; they can't do your staff work
- **Dual-status Marines** — your S-4 has a civilian job; don't assume 24/7 availability
- **Compressed training year** — 48 IDTs + 14 AT days to accomplish what active units do in 365
- **Geographic dispersion** — Marines may drive 2+ hours to drill; admin errors cost them a wasted trip

## Staff Section Quick Reference

### S-1 (Administration / Personnel)

**Core duties:**
- Muster reporting and accountability
- Drill Manager attendance entry and certification
- MROWS order writing (AT, ADT, ADSW)
- DTS oversight — ensure vouchers get filed
- Casualty reporting procedures (know them cold even if you never use them)
- Awards processing and tracking
- Promotion package management

**Reserve-specific:**
- Track Marines approaching 20-year sanctuary or high-year tenure
- Monitor IDT travel reimbursement against MARADMIN caps
- Coordinate with I&I admin chief on Unit Diary backlog
- Maintain a "who's on orders" tracker for any given month

### S-2 (Intelligence)

**Core duties:**
- Intelligence preparation of the battlespace (IPB) for exercises
- Threat assessment and force protection awareness
- Collection management and PIR tracking
- Security manager duties (clearance tracking, access rosters)

**Reserve-specific:**
- Maintain clearance tracker — know who's current, who's expired, who's in adjudication
- OSINT-based threat updates for AT locations
- Brief travel advisories for Marines traveling to drill sites or AT locations
- Coordinate with higher G-2 for exercise scenario support

### S-3 (Operations / Training)

**Core duties:**
- Training schedule development and publication
- Range and training area coordination
- Exercise planning and execution
- Training readiness reporting
- ORM worksheet development for all training events

**Reserve-specific:**
- Build training schedules 30+ days out — reservists need advance notice
- Coordinate with site support (ranges, classrooms, simulators) well ahead
- Track individual training requirements (rifle qual, PFT/CFT, MOS schools)
- Plan AT 6+ months out — MROWS needs lead time, Marines need employer notice
- Maintain a training event tracker with status (planned/resourced/executed)

### S-4 (Logistics)

**Core duties:**
- Supply accountability and inventory management
- Equipment readiness reporting
- Chow/billeting coordination for drill weekends
- Transportation planning
- Ammunition requests and accountability

**Reserve-specific:**
- Equipment in reserve armories degrades between drills — schedule PME/PMCS
- Coordinate with I&I supply chief for GCSS-MC transactions
- Ammo requests have long lead times — submit early
- Vehicle dispatch requires I&I coordination (POV vs. govt vehicle policies)

### S-6 (Communications)

**Core duties:**
- Communications planning (PACE plans)
- Radio and IT equipment accountability
- COMSEC management (if applicable — handle with care)
- Network/email coordination

**Reserve-specific:**
- Radios sit in cages between drills — PMI/battery checks are critical
- Many reserve units rely on cell phones and personal email more than tactical comms
- Coordinate with I&I for NIPR/SIPR access during drill weekends
- Ensure SMCR-specific distribution lists stay current

### XO (Executive Officer)

**Core duties:**
- Internal coordination of all staff sections
- Training meeting chair
- Readiness reporting oversight
- Staff officer development and mentoring

**Reserve-specific:**
- The XO is the continuity engine — if the XO doesn't track it, it dies between drills
- Run a due-out tracker that survives the 28-day gap
- Chair a pre-drill sync call (phone/Teams) at D-7 to catch problems early
- Own the staff estimate process for exercises

### 1stSgt / SgtMaj

**Core duties:**
- Accountability and good order/discipline
- Formation management
- Enlisted career guidance
- Commander's eyes and ears

**Reserve-specific:**
- The 1stSgt often knows more about the Marines' situations than anyone else
- Coordinate liberty plan and travel safety for Marines driving long distances
- Track Marines at risk of administrative separation for non-participation
- Bridge between I&I and drilling Marines on personnel issues

*UNCLASSIFIED — advisory reference. Adapt to your unit's specific organization and SOP.*
