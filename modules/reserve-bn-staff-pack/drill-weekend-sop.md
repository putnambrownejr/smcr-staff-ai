# Drill Weekend Standard Operating Procedure (UNCLASSIFIED)

## Pre-Drill (D-14 to D-1)

### D-14
- [ ] Training schedule published and distributed to all hands
- [ ] Range/facility reservations confirmed (call POC, don't just email)
- [ ] Ammo request status verified (if live-fire)
- [ ] Guest instructor / subject matter expert confirmed (if applicable)

### D-7
- [ ] Chow coordination finalized (headcount to messhall or caterer)
- [ ] Billeting confirmed (if overnight or AT)
- [ ] Transportation plan finalized (govt vehicles dispatched, POV guidance sent)
- [ ] Admin readiness check: any Marines with pay issues, expired PHA, missing orders?
- [ ] S-1 verifies: who's on orders, who's excused, who's UA-risk?
- [ ] Equipment/supply pre-staged or draw plan confirmed with I&I

### D-3
- [ ] Safety brief / ORM worksheet published
- [ ] Weather check — adjust plan if outdoor training is affected
- [ ] Gear list reminder sent to all hands
- [ ] Pre-execution checks: radios charged, classrooms reserved, ammo counted

### D-1
- [ ] XO confirms all staff sections are ready (quick round-robin call or text)
- [ ] Duty driver and corpsman assigned (if applicable)
- [ ] Key leader contact info current (cell phones, not just govt email)
- [ ] Muster sheet printed or Drill Manager ready for digital entry

## During Drill

### Formation / Accountability (first 30 minutes)
1. 1stSgt takes accountability by section/platoon
2. Report muster to S-1 within 30 minutes of first formation
3. Account for excused absences (approved in advance by CO)
4. Flag any unexpected no-shows — attempt contact before marking UA

### Training Execution
1. S-3 manages training schedule adherence — keep events on time
2. Designated safety officer monitors risk controls per ORM worksheet
3. Document training completion (sign-in rosters for every event)
4. Photograph training for COMMSTRAT / unit social media (with release)

### Admin Windows
1. S-1 runs admin table: ID card issues, MOL access help, award submissions
2. Career planner available for PME/reenlistment counseling (if assigned)
3. Medical/dental — schedule PHA catch-ups if Navy medical is present
4. DTS help — walk Marines through voucher filing if they're behind

### Commander Decision Points
1. End of Saturday: training assessment — adjust Sunday plan if needed
2. End of drill: approve training schedule for next month
3. Sign awards, FitRep endorsements, and correspondence stacked during drill

## Post-Drill (Release + 5 business days)

### Immediate (Release Day)
- [ ] Accountability — confirm all Marines departed safely
- [ ] Equipment/ammo returned and secured
- [ ] Facility policed and keys returned
- [ ] Range cleared (if applicable)

### Within 5 Business Days
- [ ] Drill Manager: attendance entered and certified
- [ ] DTS: remind Marines to file travel vouchers
- [ ] MROWS: submit any orders for upcoming ADT/AT
- [ ] AAR: draft sustains/improves and file in shared drive
- [ ] Training completion: update individual training records
- [ ] Due-out tracker: update status of all open items
- [ ] Handoff notes: capture anything the next drill needs to know

## Common Drill Weekend Problems and Quick Fixes

| Problem | Quick Fix |
|---------|-----------|
| Marine shows up without CAC | Use MOL on I&I computer for temp access; submit RAPIDS appointment |
| Pay discrepancy discovered | Check LES on MOL; cross-reference Drill Manager entries; flag to I&I admin |
| Range goes cold unexpectedly | Have alternate classroom training ready (the "hip pocket" class) |
| Key instructor is a no-show | S-3 backfills from officer/SNCO pool; have backup lesson plans |
| Marine needs emergency leave | 1stSgt routes through CO; S-1 processes Red Cross message if applicable |
| Equipment failure during training | S-4 coordinates with I&I maintenance; document in GCSS-MC |
| Weather cancels outdoor training | Execute rain plan; use indoor facilities; don't waste the drill day |

*UNCLASSIFIED — advisory SOP template. Adapt to your unit's specific requirements and command guidance.*
