                                                                                Workflow lane

                                                                                Use this lane when you need to build products, run planning support, pressure-test a brief, or cover gaps outside your normal box.

                                                                                Which tool do I use?

                                                                                20 min, cold, no staff → Lone Planner Mode. Gets you a walk-in brief and blind spots fast.

                                                                                30+ min, clear mission goal → Planning Cell. Full mission analysis, assumptions, decisions, due-outs.

                                                                                Missing sections → Section Gap Cover. Builds what each absent section would have said.

                                                                                Briefing the command group → Staff Package. XO, admin, safety, SEL, medical — one integrated output.

                                                                                Drill weekend, sections checking in → Staff Update Cycle. Feed section updates, get a running estimate and CUB.

                                                                                Brief is drafted, needs tightening → Brief Clinic. Paste your draft, get pressure on logic and decision clarity.

                                                                                Between drills, continuity → Battle Rhythm Editor. Update assumptions, decisions, questions, due-outs so the next session isn't a cold start.

                                                                                Start Here

                                                                                Use the shortest path back into the problem before you start building supporting products.

                                                                                Start here if you're alone

                                                                                Lone Planner Mode

                                                                                You're covering multiple lanes with limited time. Gives you a walk-in brief, the blind spots you'd miss, and what to ask each absent section. Takes ~2 minutes.

                                                                                Start here if you have a mission

                                                                                Planning Cell

                                                                                You have a clear training goal and 30+ minutes. Builds a full mission analysis — assumptions, decision points, due-outs, and section coordination — in one package.

                                                                                Start here if you're briefing the CO

                                                                                Integrated Staff Package

                                                                                Builds a complete staff output across XO, command cell, S-1/Admin, Safety, SEL, and Medical in one pass. Use when the command group needs a full picture, not just the S-3 lane.

                                                                                Use after planning — not before

                                                                                Brief Clinic

                                                                                Your brief is drafted but it needs to get sharper. Paste the current draft and it will pressure the logic, audience fit, and decision clarity. Not a planning tool — a sharpening tool.

                                                                                Battle Rhythm Editor

                                                                                Update the persistent board here when assumptions, decisions, questions, or due-outs need a real edit.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                What it's for: Continuity between drills. Fill this out after each drill and it prevents a cold start next time.

                                                                                Commander focus: What the commander wants protected or prioritized — in their words, not staff language.

                                                                                Assumption log: Things you're treating as true but haven't confirmed. Format: S-4: Transport window remains workable.

                                                                                Decision log: Calls already made. Records why you're doing what you're doing. Format: Command: Approved one primary training lane.

                                                                                Question log: Open questions that need answers before next drill. Format: S-6: What access issue is still unresolved?

                                                                                Due-outs: Items someone owns. Format: S-4: Confirm transport timeline before next sync.

                                                                                Tip: Planning Cell can save directly here — use "Save to battle rhythm board" after running a planning cell.

                                                                                Personnel products

                                                                                FitRep, awards, and routing-package support.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: You're building or reviewing a FitRep, award package, or routing package and want a structural scaffold.

                                                                                What you get: Section structure, routing steps, review checklist, and required document list — not the writing itself.

                                                                                FitRep planning vs bullets: Planning gives you a preparation checklist and timeline. Bullets gives you a scaffold for the actual performance traits.

                                                                                Subject field: Use a generic description (e.g., "Capt, civil affairs, BN XO billet") — no real names or SSNs.

                                                                                Generated sections, routing steps, and review points will appear here.

                                                                                Staff products

                                                                                Fast scaffolds for orders, correspondence, reports, and AARs.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: You need the correct format and section structure for a product, not a blank page.

                                                                                What you get: Section headers, required content checklist, and review points for the selected product type.

                                                                                WARNO / OPORD / FRAGO: Planning orders. Use WARNO early, OPORD when the plan is set, FRAGO for changes.

                                                                                SITREP: Situation report scaffold — use for routine or event-driven status reporting.

                                                                                Naval letter / Memo / Endorsement: Correspondence formats. The tool gives you the correct header structure and routing guidance.

                                                                                AAR: After action review — observations, discussions, and lessons in the standard format.

                                                                                Sections and review notes will appear here.

                                                                                Brief Clinic

                                                                                Tighten a CUB, decision brief, command update, or civil-affairs CPB around audience, decision, evidence, and backup.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: You already have a draft brief. This is a sharpening tool, not a planning tool.

                                                                                Decision required field: Be specific — "Approve the training concept" is better than "brief the commander." If you can't name the decision, the brief isn't ready.

                                                                                Paste your draft: Put whatever you have — an outline, bullet list, or full slides summary. The clinic will pressure the logic and flag what the audience will ask first.

                                                                                CUB vs CPB: CUB = Command Update Brief (internal, to the commander). CPB = Civil-Military Planning Board brief (external or interagency audience).

                                                                                Brief-clinic feedback will appear here.

                                                                                Uniform Photo Review

                                                                                Upload a local uniform photo and run a source-anchored advisory review against the uniform order.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: Pre-drill inspection prep, ceremony uniform check, or when you have a specific wear question you want to run against MCO 1020.34H.

                                                                                What it does: Gives you a source-anchored checklist of what to verify in the image — it does NOT perform automated AI image analysis. The review framework is the value, not computer vision.

                                                                                Visible items: List what's actually visible in the photo (rank insignia, ribbons/badges, cover, belt/buckle, footwear, grooming/hair).

                                                                                Photo is stored locally only — it stays on your machine, not sent to any external service.

                                                                                Ambiguous calls: The output will tell you to route unresolved questions through the chain of command or SEL.

                                                                                Photo-assisted uniform review will appear here, anchored to MCO 1020.34H and official follow-on links.

                                                                                MOS advisor

                                                                                Run a focused staff MOS lane when you need the narrower officer slice under S-1, S-3, or S-4.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: You're covering a lane that's not your primary MOS and need to know what that officer would prioritize.

                                                                                0102 Adjutant: Admin accountability, correspondence, awards, and reserve admin continuity.

                                                                                0402 Logistics: Supportability, sustainment judgment, lead times, and what breaks the plan first.

                                                                                3002 Supply: Supply accountability, fiscal discipline, inventory readiness, and command supply risk.

                                                                                0511 MAGTF Planner: Mission analysis, planning support, assumption control, and staff integration.

                                                                                Prompt tip: Describe the specific problem or question — "Help me tighten the logistics estimate for AT" works better than "what should I do."

                                                                                MOS-specific advisory output will appear here.

                                                                                Staff update cycle

                                                                                Turn quick section updates into a running estimate, CUB, and decision brief without rebuilding the frame.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: Drill weekend — sections are checking in and you need to roll their updates into a structured output without rebuilding the planning cell from scratch.

                                                                                Section updates format: One line per section: S-3: Concept is supportable if the company cuts to one primary lane.

                                                                                What you get: A running estimate, CUB structure, and decision brief scaffold based on what each section said.

                                                                                Difference from Planning Cell: The update cycle assumes planning is done — it processes status, not analysis. Use the planning cell for analysis, the update cycle for drill-weekend sync.

                                                                                Running estimate, CUB, and decision-brief outputs will appear here.

                                                                                Planning Cell

                                                                                Run mission analysis, pick a planning rhythm, and keep assumptions, decisions, and due-outs visible.
                                                                                How to use this

                                                                                When: You have a training goal, 30+ minutes, and need a structured planning package — not just notes.

                                                                                Mission / training goal: The one thing the event must accomplish. Be specific.

                                                                                Commander priorities: What to protect even if the plan has to shrink. One per line.

                                                                                Higher guidance: What battalion or higher has told you. Constraints go in the Constraints field — these are two different things.

                                                                                Section updates: What each section knows right now. Format: S-4: Transport is available but timing is tight.

                                                                                What you get: Mission analysis, planning approach, assumption log, decision log, and due-outs — ready to brief or save to the Battle Rhythm Board.

                                                                                Lone Planner vs Section Gap Cover: Run Lone Planner first if you're cold. Run Section Gap Cover after the planning cell if you're missing sections and need to fill the gaps.

                                                                                Mission analysis, planning approach, assumption log, decision log, and due-outs will appear here.
                                                                                Lone planner assist will surface walk-in brief lines, blind spots, cross-lane asks, and immediate actions here.
                                                                                Assisted section estimates will surface likely questions, support facts, coordination, and XO-ready walk-in lines here.
                                                                                The integrated staff package will surface planning posture, command decisions, risk cuts, section outputs, and linked staff products here.
