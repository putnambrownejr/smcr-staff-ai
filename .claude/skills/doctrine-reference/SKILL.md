# Doctrine Reference — smcr-staff-ai

## Purpose

Quick lookup of the right doctrinal publication for a topic. Agents cite specific references in `source_refs.py`, but users often need to find a publication by topic rather than by number. This skill bridges that gap.

## When to Use

- "What pub covers fires coordination?"
- "What's the MCDP for command and control?"
- "Which MCWP should I read for legal support?"
- "What joint pub governs logistics?"
- When adding source references to an agent and need to identify the right publication
- When a user asks about doctrine and you need to point them to the authoritative source

## Publication Hierarchy

Marine Corps publications follow a tiered hierarchy:

| Tier | Prefix | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capstone** | MCDP | Foundational philosophy and warfighting concepts | MCDP 1 Warfighting |
| **Warfighting** | MCWP | How to execute warfighting functions | MCWP 3-31 Fires and Effects |
| **Tactics/Techniques** | MCTP | Detailed procedures and staff processes | MCTP 3-30A MAGTF Staff Training |
| **Reference** | MCRP | Reference data, organizational information | MCRP 1-10.1 Organization of the USMC |
| **Joint** | JP | Joint doctrine across services | JP 2-0 Joint Intelligence |

## Capstone Doctrine (MCDP)

These are the foundational publications every Marine officer should know:

| Publication | Title | What it governs |
|---|---|---|
| **MCDP 1** | Warfighting | Marine Corps philosophy — maneuver warfare, combined arms, mission tactics, commander's intent. The intellectual foundation for everything else. |
| **MCDP 1-0** | Marine Corps Operations | MAGTF employment across the range of military operations. Defines MAGTF structure (CE/GCE/ACE/LCE). |
| **MCDP 1-3** | Tactics | Application of combat power to defeat an enemy at a particular time and place. Art and science of winning engagements and battles. |
| **MCDP 2** | Intelligence | Doctrinal foundation for Marine intelligence. Philosophy of intelligence support. |
| **MCDP 3** | Expeditionary Operations | MEF/MEB/MEU employment. Echeloned deployment, 60-day sustainment, naval integration. |
| **MCDP 4** | Logistics | Marine logistics philosophy. Sustainment as a warfighting function. |
| **MCDP 6** | Command and Control | Theory and philosophy of reaching effective decisions and implementing effective actions faster than an adversary. |

## Modernization and Force Design Documents

These are not traditional doctrine but shape current force employment:

| Document | What it governs |
|---|---|
| **Force Design 2030** (and annual updates) | Structural transformation — distributed littoral ops, stand-in forces, MLR, divestiture of legacy formations, loitering munitions, sUAS, EW/SIGINT, counter-sUAS. |
| **Installations and Logistics 2030** | Contested sustainment, diversified distribution, installation readiness. |
| **Training and Education 2030** | Modernized training environment, LVC, AI literacy, integrated kill webs, unmanned systems. |
| **Talent Management 2030** | Personnel reform — talent alignment, retention, lateral moves, technical skill management. |
| **2025/2026 Marine Corps Aviation Plans** | F-35B/C transition, CH-53K deployment, MQ-9A growth, distributed aviation operations, decision-centric aviation operations. |

## Statutory Authority

| Authority | What it governs |
|---|---|
| **10 U.S.C. § 8063** | Statutory functions of the Marine Corps — organized, trained, and equipped for service with the fleet, seizure/defense of advanced naval bases, land operations essential to naval campaigns. Explains why Marine organization remains sea-based, expeditionary, and combined-arms. |

## How to Use This Skill

### User asks "what pub covers X?"

1. Identify the functional area from the tables above
2. Start with the capstone MCDP if they want philosophy/foundations
3. Point to the specific MCWP/MCTP/MCRP for practical application
4. Note the joint pub if their question involves joint context
5. Cite the publication number, title, and one-sentence description of what it governs

### Agent needs a source reference

When adding or updating `source_refs.py` entries:
1. Look up the publication in the tables above
2. Use the official title format: "MCO 1610.7B Performance Evaluation System"
3. Find the URL on marines.mil MCPEL (Marine Corps Publications Electronic Library)
4. Publications change — check the MCPEL for the current version before citing

### User asks about doctrine changes

Point them to Force Design 2030 and the annual updates for structural change, or to the specific publication for their functional area. Note that MCRP 1-10.1 explicitly states it no longer contains specific structure information — that's in the CAC-restricted TFSMS.

## Rules

- **Publication numbers change.** MCOs get revised, MCWPs get new edition letters. Always verify against the MCPEL before treating a number as current.
- **Don't reproduce full publications.** These are references and pointers, not substitutes for reading the actual doctrine.
- **UNCLASSIFIED only.** All publications referenced here are publicly available through the MCPEL or official .mil sites. Do not reference CAC-restricted or classified publications.
- **Doctrine lags experimentation.** Current force structure and capabilities may not yet be reflected in published doctrine, especially below battalion and for Force Design formations like the MLR.
